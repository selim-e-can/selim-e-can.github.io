About Me

Hi! My name is Selim Emir Can, and I am a Senior at UCLA studying Electrical Engineering. I was born and raised in Istanbul, Turkey but moved to Corning, New York during my sophomore year of high school school. I am indebted to my high school teachers for shaping my broad interests in STEM, teaching me lessons applicable to situations far beyond the laboratory or classroom, and indebted to encouraging friends who, just like me, are all passionate about engineering and science. Likewise, my professors at UCLA have allowed me to develop a completely new appreciation for engineering, math and pyhsics which I am very grateful for.

I am interested in the field of computer vision and computational imaging and their applications in medical devices and robotics. I currently conduct research in the Visual Machines Group. Outside of classes, I like to attend Computer Vision seminars hosted by my lab and fulfill my position as a board member (Historian) of Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) at UCLA. I also tutor for free, so feel free to reach me at emir2903@gmail.com for any questions! Outside of school, I am very passionate about sports. I love playing table tennis, basketball, and run cross country.
